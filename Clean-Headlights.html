The blog article recommends a seven-step process for properly washing a car.

 

Step 1: Set up.

Park the car out of direct sunlight, and make sure all supplies are near the vehicle. Use two buckets — one for washing and one for rinsing. Remove belts, jewelry, watches, or rings before cleaning the car. Ideally, the car owner will wash the entire automobile in one session, which should take about an hour, depending on the size of the vehicle and how dirty it is.

Step 2: Prewash the car.

Hose down the automobile to remove the worst of any dirt buildup or debris. Use a gentle stream of warm to avoid scratching the vehicle.

Step 3: Clean the wheels and tires first.

Use a spray-on, wipe-off cleaner, and let it sit for a bit. After hosing off the cleaner, scrub the tires in a circular motion to remove the rest of the dirt and grime. Clean the tighter areas with a sudsy wheel sponge, and use a smaller scrub brush to clean inside the wheels, if necessary. Always rinse out the wheel wash bucket thoroughly before using it for the regular wash.

Step 4: Wash using the two-bucket system.

One bucket is filled with car shampoo, and the other bucket is filled with clean water for rinsing out the microfiber cleaning mitt. Wash the car in sections, starting from the top down and hosing down each section after it is cleaned. Regularly clean the mitt in the rinse bucket, and refill the bucket with clean water as needed.

Step 5: Dry the car.

Wipe down all surfaces with a microfiber cloth to prevent rust from building up.

Step 6: Use a detailing mitt or clay bar.

This step is usually only necessary once or twice a year or just before a car polish or ceramic coating.

Step 7: Polish the car.

Only polish as needed, which is usually during a full detail once or twice a year. Use a buffer for best results. A buffer distributes pressure more evenly than a manual pad and offers different speed settings.
